Introduction

Condensed state physics is the field of physics that deals with the macroscopic and microscopic physical properties of matter. In particular, it is concerned with the "condensed" phases that appear whenever the number of constituents in a system is extremely large and the interactions between the constituents are strong.

Applications

Condensed state physics has a wide range of applications, including the development of new materials, understanding the properties of liquids and solids, and the study of phenomena such as superconductivity and magnetism.

Research Areas

  • Superconductivity
  • Magnetism
  • Quantum Mechanics
  • Nanotechnology
  • Material Science
